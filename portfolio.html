What drives wine complexity?

The aim this study was to look at how wine complexity varying with type and age may perceived differently between novices and expert tasters. The method that was used here was Temporal Check All That Apply which means that participants were asked to provide their temporal perception of the wine. We also looked on what characteristics of the wine drives complexity. The results of this study is published in Food Quality and Preference in 2021.

Factors Levels Dimensions Responses
Wine type 3 TCATA 8 attributes over 120 seconds
Age 3 (partial sampling) Wine meta-factors 5
Exploring HPP processing parameters and its influences chemical properties of lamb cuts

One of the aim of this study was to understand the relationship free amino acids and fatty acids with TBARS. In a nutshell, the higher the TBARS value, the more oxidation species and flavours would appear in the products. The results of this study is published in Foods in 2020.

Changes in flavour, emotion, and electrophysiological measures in different eating environments

The aim of the study is to see how changes in emotion both measured subjectively and objectively (e.g. heart rate) can influece changes in flavour perception in different environmental context. The results of this study is published in Food Quality and Preference in 2019.

Do we eat more while we work in front of a computer?

We were interested to see if we eat more pizza while we work in front of our computer, this was definitely one of that fun study as pizza was provided ad-libitum to the participants! The results of this study is published in Nutrients in 2019.
